Aircraft

    Destruction of aircraft or aircraft facilities

    Severe penalties for deliberately damaging or endangering aircraft or facilities in the United States or abroad. Acts like damaging aircraft, interfering with navigation facilities, or endangering passengers could lead to fines, imprisonment for up to 20 years, or both. Threats aimed at carrying out these actions, punishable by fines, imprisonment for up to 5 years, or both.

    Violence and international airports

    Violent acts at international airports, including causing serious harm to individuals or damaging airport facilities, which endanger safety come with consiquences. These criminals can face fines, imprisonment up to 20 years, or both. If death results, the punishment can include the death penalty or life imprisonment.

    Fraud

    This law deals with fraud involving aircraft or space vehicle parts in interstate or foreign commerce. It prohibits various fraudulent acts and states penalties. Punishments range from fines to imprisonment up to 20 years or life if the fraud causes serious injury or death. Organizations committing such offenses can receive fines up to $20,000,000. It also applies to U.S. citizens or residents regardless of where the offense occurs, provided it involves U.S.-owned aircraft or space vehicle parts.

    Aiming a lazer pointer at an aircraft

    This law makes it illegal to aim a laser pointer at an aircraft or its flight path. If someone does this knowingly, they could be fined or put in jail for up to 5 years, or both. However, there are some exceptions. It's okay for authorized people doing certain jobs, like research or emergency signaling, to use laser pointers near aircraft. The Attorney General can make more rules about when it's okay to use laser pointers around planes if needed, after talking to certain committees in the government.

    Unsafe operation of unmanned aircraft

    This law says it's against the rules to operate a drone in a way that could cause danger to airplanes or people inside them. If someone does this on purpose, they could be fined or put in jail for up to a year. If they cause serious harm or try to, the punishment could be much more severe, including a longer jail time or even life imprisonment. It's also illegal to fly a drone close to certain parts of an airport without permission.
